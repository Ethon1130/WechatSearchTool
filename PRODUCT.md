# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Researchers, operators, founders, analysts, and content teams who need to extract useful information from WeChat public-account articles and ordinary webpages. They are usually in a research workflow: collecting source links, checking article metadata, summarizing content, comparing multiple sources, and exporting reusable findings.

## Product Purpose

WechatResearchTool helps users turn public articles and webpages into structured research material. It extracts titles, sources, authors, publish times, body text, images, links, optional AI summaries, and merged research reports. Success means users can move from scattered URLs to reliable, exportable research notes with minimal manual cleanup.

## Brand Personality

Research-oriented, clean, trustworthy, and refined. The interface should feel calm and capable: a practical tool for serious information work, not a flashy marketing surface.

## Anti-references

Do not make the product look like a decorative landing page, a dark hacker console, or a page full of ornamental cards. Avoid gradient color systems and gradient accents. Avoid loud, saturated decoration that competes with reading, extraction, and review tasks.

## Design Principles

1. Put research workflow first: primary actions, source content, summaries, and exports should be easy to find without visual noise.
2. Preserve trust through restraint: use clear hierarchy, reliable spacing, readable contrast, and consistent controls.
3. Make dense information scan naturally: metadata, candidates, summaries, and reports should group by task and meaning.
4. Keep the surface fresh but quiet: add quality through alignment, rhythm, typography, and refined neutral surfaces instead of decoration.
5. Design for repeat use: the layout should feel efficient during long sessions, batch work, and iterative research.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use ordinary product accessibility standards: clear contrast, keyboard-reachable controls, visible focus states, mobile layouts that do not overflow, and no unnecessary motion. If motion is introduced later, it should respect reduced-motion preferences.
